Global Estimation of Dietary Micronutrient Inadequacies — National & Subnational Prevalence Estimates

Modeled national and subnational prevalence estimates of inadequate micronutrient intake (15 micronutrients including calcium, iron, zinc, vitamin B12, iodine, etc.) for most countries worldwide, including Saudi Arabia, based on the modeling analysis published in The Lancet Global Health (Passarelli et al. 2024). Includes the full companion GitHub repository (code, data inputs, figures, tables, and Shiny app source) as archived locally.

Known issues: Estimates are model-based (probability/EAR-cut-point approaches applied to GDD and GENuS dietary intake distributions), not direct biomarker measurements.; No sub-national or Red-Sea-specific spatial resolution beyond the repository's own admin-1 subnational estimates; Saudi Arabia's subnational units do not align with Red Sea subbasin boundaries, so all five subbasins are tagged with the national/subnational figures uniformly.; Repository also contains large binary R data (.Rds) outputs and manuscript drafts not itemized as resources here; only the two primary tabular outputs and the metadata workbook are cataloged.; License terms are not explicitly stated in the repository; publicly hosted alongside an open-access Lancet Global Health article but redistribution terms should be confirmed with the authors before reuse.
